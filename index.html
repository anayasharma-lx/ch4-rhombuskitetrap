Rhombus: All four sides equal. The diagonals bisect each other at 90°, and each diagonal bisects its two vertex angles. ∠A = ∠C, ∠B = ∠D, ∠A + ∠B = 180° A rhombus is a parallelogram — so it inherits all parallelogram properties too.

Kite: Two adjacent pairs of equal sides (AB=BC, CD=DA). The "main" diagonal (from A to C) bisects the other diagonal at 90°, and bisects the angles at A and C. One pair of opposite angles (∠B = ∠D) are equal. A kite is NOT a parallelogram (opposite sides are not equal).

Trapezium: At least one pair of parallel sides. Co-interior angles on the same side add to 180°. ∠P + ∠S = 180°, ∠Q + ∠R = 180° In an isosceles trapezium, the two non-parallel sides are equal, and the base angles are equal (∠P = ∠Q, ∠R = ∠S).

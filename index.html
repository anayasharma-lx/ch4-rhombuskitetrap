Chapter 4 · Quadrilaterals

Rhombus, Kite & Trapezium

Adjust the sliders and watch the diagonals, angles, and parallel sides respond to each shape's rules. The constraints are built in — you can't break them.

Rhombus: all four sides equal. The diagonals bisect each other at exactly 90° and each bisects its vertex angles. It is a parallelogram — so opposite angles are equal and adjacent angles sum to 180°.
Side length 100
Angle ∠A 60°
All four sides = one slider. Angle ∠A sets everything: ∠C = ∠A, ∠B = ∠D = 180° − ∠A. The diagonals always cross at 90° — try making ∠A = 90° and the rhombus becomes a square.
Kite: two pairs of adjacent sides equal (AB = AD, CB = CD). The main diagonal AC is a line of symmetry — it bisects BD at 90° and bisects the angles at A and C. One pair of opposite angles (∠B = ∠D) are equal.
AB = AD (top) 80
CB = CD (bottom) 120
Half-width at A 55
Drag the half-width to see the kite widen and narrow. The 90° crossing of the diagonals is always preserved — that's the kite's defining diagonal property. Notice ∠B always equals ∠D.
Trapezium: at least one pair of parallel sides (PQ ∥ SR). Co-interior angles on the same side always add to 180°: ∠P + ∠S = 180° and ∠Q + ∠R = 180°. In an isosceles trapezium the legs are equal and base angles are equal.
Top PQ 100
Bottom SR 180
Base angle ∠S 70°
Notice that ∠P + ∠S is always exactly 180° no matter how you move the sliders — that's the co-interior angle rule for parallel lines. Tick isosceles and watch both legs become equal and both pairs of base angles equalise.
